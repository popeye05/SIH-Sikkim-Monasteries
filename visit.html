A Journey to the Sacred Heart of the Himalayas

Your guide to a respectful and enriching pilgrimage to the monasteries of Sikkim.

Planning Your Journey

Getting to Sikkim

By Air: The nearest major airport is Bagdogra (IXB) in West Bengal. Pakyong (PYG) is Sikkim's own airport with limited connectivity.
By Rail: New Jalpaiguri (NJP) is the closest major railway station.
By Road: Taxis and buses are available from Bagdogra, NJP, and Siliguri.

Best Time to Visit

Mar-May (Spring): Pleasant weather and clear views of the mountains.
Oct-Dec (Autumn): Crisp air, sunny days, and major festivals.
Jun-Sep (Monsoon): Lush greenery, but be prepared for rain and potential landslides.

Permits & Regulations

Foreign nationals require a Restricted Area Permit (RAP). All visitors, including domestic tourists, need a Protected Area Permit (PAP) for areas like Tsomgo Lake, North Sikkim, etc. Ensure you have valid ID proofs.

Sacred Etiquette: A Visitor's Guide

✓

Dress Modestly

Cover shoulders and knees when entering monastic premises.

✓

Circumambulate Clockwise

Always walk around stupas, temples, and prayer wheels in a clockwise direction (parikrama).

✓

Ask Before Photographing

Respect privacy, especially of monks. Photography is often prohibited inside prayer halls.

✗

Do Not Touch Artifacts

Refrain from touching statues, murals, thangkas, or ritual objects.

✗

Avoid Loud Noises

Maintain silence and serenity within the monastery grounds.

✗

Do Not Point Feet

When sitting, avoid pointing the soles of your feet towards altars or revered lamas.

Suggested Itineraries

The Heritage Trail (5 Days)

A journey through Sikkim's most historically significant monasteries, including Rumtek, Pemayangtse, and Tashiding.

A Spiritual Retreat (7 Days)

An immersive experience focusing on monasteries that offer teachings and quiet contemplation in serene environments.

Festival Chaser's Guide

Plan your trip around vibrant monastic festivals like Losar (New Year) or the sacred masked dances (Cham).
